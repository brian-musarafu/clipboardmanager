// what it does

Everything you copy, at your fingertips.

Built with SwiftUI, SwiftData, and Swift 6 — fast, native, and private by design.

🗂️

Clipboard history

Automatic capture of text, images, and files. Search, pin, and favourite; filter by type. Click to paste into the focused app, drag anywhere, and it survives restarts.

⌨️

Shortcuts & snippets

Summon the quick panel with ⌘⇧V from anywhere, paste the top pinned item with ⌘⇧1, and expand snippet triggers like /sig in any app — with variables like {{date}}.

✨

Smart clipboard

On-device actions on any text: open & shorten links, compose email, colour swatches, pretty-print JSON, generate QR codes, plus AI language detection, summaries, and Apple Translate.

🔎

Images & OCR

Extract text from any image with on-device Vision, then copy it straight out or save it to your history — no uploads, no round-trips.

🔐

Security & privacy

Password-manager-concealed content is never captured. Card numbers and SSNs are detected, redacted, and AES-256 encrypted; revealing a secret needs Touch ID. Private Mode pauses tracking. No analytics, no network calls.

💾

Backup & sync

Export history and snippets to JSON, or history to CSV (secrets excluded). Import merges and skips duplicates. iCloud sync is code-ready for when you want it.

// get started

Download & install.

Under a minute from download to first paste. Clipio is distributed directly (not via the Mac App Store), like other paste-capable clipboard managers.

1

Download the disk image

Grab Clipio.dmg (~940 KB) using the button below, then double-click it to open.

2

Drag Clipio to Applications

In the window that opens, drag the Clipio icon into your Applications folder.

3

Open it the first time

Right-click (or Control-click) Clipio in Applications → Open → Open. This one-time step is needed because Clipio isn’t notarized through the App Store.

4

Grant Accessibility access

Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and enable Clipio. This lets it paste into other apps and run text expansion. You’ll only be asked once.

5

You’re set

Clipio lives in the menu bar (no Dock icon). Press ⌘⇧V to open the panel. Optionally turn on Launch at Login from the menu.

Requirements: macOS 14 Sonoma or later, on Apple silicon or Intel. Clipio runs outside the App Sandbox (required for pasting into other apps) — which is why it’s a direct download. Touch ID is used only when you reveal a protected secret.
